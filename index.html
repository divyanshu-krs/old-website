Research Projects

Research Projects

"Hey! Let us record this!" : Investigating Pereception of School Students towards Social Robots in India Jan 2020-Present

The aim of this project is to study the first-hand perception of school students and teacher in New Delhi, India using quantitative as well as qualitative methods. Most of the work in Human-Robot Interacion(HRI) is being coducted in the Global North, hence it extremely important to understand the interaction paradigms in the Global South. With advancement in the technology for education, Indian schools are gradually adopting these techs. Therefore, to understand the interaction with social robots, we conducted interaction study with 33 students from two different Delhi Govt. schools and to better understand the behaviour of students we also, introduced the social robot to their teachers as well.
To be submitted at ACM Conference on Supported Cooperative Work and Social Computing 2020 .
Team Member : Pramil Panjwani (Ph.D Scholar IIIT Delhi).
Advisor : Dr. Jainendra Shukla

Predicting Anxiety in Adults using EDA and HRV Signals
Jan 2020- Present

The aim of this project is to develop a MachineLearning(ML) model using both EDA & HRV signals, to predict patterns of anxiety in adults. Novelty of this works lies in thefact that there are no pre-existing model in ML which uses these two modalities to deduce anxiety.


Team Member(s) : Tanya Budhiraja (Undergrad IIIT Delhu); Pramil Pranjawani (Ph.D Scholar IIIT Delhi).
Advisor(s) : Dr. Mrinmoy Chakrabarty ; Dr. Jainendra Shukla .

A Survey on the Perception of Adults with Autism Spectrum Disorder: Roles and Benefits of Social Robots in the Therapy Jul 2019-Present

The aim of the project is to conduct a survey/interview to obtain theperception of adults with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in relation to the therapies and/or the interventions they received and their view and suggestions on the use of social robots for robot-assisted interventions for the cognitive well-being of thechildren with ASD.


Advisor(s) : Dr. Jessica Korte ; Dr. Jainendra Shukla .; Prof. Anubha Gupta .
